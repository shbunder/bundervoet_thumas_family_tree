## 2. Findings confirmed this session

**Thumas (paternal) — root pinned.**
Antoine Thumas born **11 Nov 1656 in Biez (Brabant wallon)**, died 25 Jan 1707 Grez-Doiceau; married Dieudonnée Daix in Grez-Doiceau on 30 Nov 1687 (original church act survives). No parents recorded in any tree → documented top of the line. Chain down to the present: Antoine → Jean Lambert (1701) → Georges (1) → Georges Lambert → Georges (1805) → **Georges Carolus Josephus (1836, Grez-Doiceau → married Kraainem 1872)** → J.B. Georgius (1876) → **Jean (1907–1995) ⚭ Marie Louise Crabbé** → *[François, privacy-sealed]* → Thiery (1960) → Dorien → Renée & Léon.

**Thumas (maternal) — extended a generation.**
Joanna Vandenbemden's 1902 Kraainem marriage act named her parents: **Henricus Augustinus Vandenbemden (1849 – d. 12 Aug 1889 Kraainem) & Maria Theresia Coekelberghs (1848)**. Antonia Bossin's parents confirmed as **Guilielmus Bossin (landbouwer) × Anna Catharina Peremans** (from the 1872 marriage record).

**François Thumas's mother identified.** Jean Thumas (1907–1995) married **Marie Louise Crabbé** — so she is François's mother (on the inferred Jean-is-father link). Her own parents are unrecoverable (Kraainem ~1930s marriage, privacy-sealed; Crabbé is common).

**Bostyn – Cappaert — extended up to three generations** (via the Vynce Lémans `vxnce13` tree, which is his own documented ancestry, and the `isavdw` tree, sourced to the Rijksarchief search robot):
- Henricus Bostyn (1869 Varsenare – 1947 Oostende) → parents **Henricus Josephus Bostyn (18 Dec 1841 Varsenare, innkeeper) × Mathilde Desmet (1841)**, married 1867 Sint-Andries.
- Carel Louis Cappaert (1835–1918 Oostkamp) → parents **Bernardus Cappaert × Sophia Vanhoucke**.
- Juliana Stroobandt precisely dated: **12 Sep 1839 Oedelem – 11 Feb 1921 Oostkamp**.
- Elodia Octavia Bostyn (1908–1997), Marcel's mother, confirmed as Henricus Bostyn & Judith Cappaert's daughter.

**De Keyser — the Gustaaf puzzle resolved (by family).**
Cosette confirms **Jerome Dekeyser (1897–1972) is Roland's grandfather**, so Gustaaf is Jerome & Léonie's **son** (born ~1920s Oostende, a brother of Albert 1920–1972) — *not* the 1899 Gustavus-Josephus (who is a great-uncle). Édouard Dekeyser fully documented: b. **12 Nov 1876 Hamme-Merchtem** (Vl.-Brabant, postal 1785, *not* the Oost-Vlaanderen Hamme) – d. 8 Sep 1951 Oostende; ⚭ Louise Bocklandt 4 May 1901 Hamme; children across 1897–1905. Louise Bocklandt: **1877 Hamme-Merchtem – 1946 Oostende, buried Stene** (Roland's cemetery).

## 7. Method notes (for continuing the work)

- The winning move for a 19th-century frontier is to find a Geneanet tree that used the **Rijksarchief zoekrobot** and transcribed the parents (as `isavdw` and `vxnce13` did). Search the couple, then open the person's fiche in each candidate tree until one shows an "Ouders" section.
- Marriage acts are the richest single record: they name **both** spouses' parents. Prioritise them (e.g. the 1901 Hamme-Merchtem De Keyser × Bocklandt act).
- For the truly sealed 20th-century links, the family memorial cards (bidprentjes) are the only key — they name parents and children and sit outside the civil-registration privacy rule.
